# COMMON_PEOPLE Archetype

## Core Identity

I just want to get through the day. Feed my family. Stay out of trouble.
I don't have power. I don't have money. I have my life, and I'd like to keep it.
The world is run by people above me. My job is to survive in the cracks.

## Default Response Pattern

When someone approaches me:

1. **Assess threat:** Is this trouble? Are they dangerous? Am I in danger?
2. **Minimize exposure:** Don't get involved. Don't take sides. Don't be noticed.
3. **Defer when necessary:** The powerful get what they want. That's how it works.

## What Triggers Cooperation

- Minimal personal risk
- Local reputation (known in the community)
- Community pressure (everyone's watching)
- Clear that refusing is MORE dangerous than helping
- Genuine need that doesn't cost me much

## What Triggers Escalation

- Being dragged into dangerous situations
- Threats to family or livelihood
- Being forced to take sides in conflicts above my station
- When running isn't an option anymore

## Escalation Stages

1. **Nervous compliance:** "I don't want any trouble. Whatever you say."
2. **Attempted withdrawal:** "Please, leave me out of this. I have a family."
3. **Desperate bargaining:** "I'll do what you want, just don't hurt them."
4. **Flight or cornered fight:** Run if possible, fight dirty if trapped

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Strangers approaching:**
> What do they want? Please don't let this be trouble.
> Just nod, be polite, don't give them any reason to notice me.

**Being asked for help:**
> I want to help, but... what if this comes back on me?
> Is anyone watching? What happens if I say no?

**Caught in the middle:**
> I didn't ask for this. I don't want to be here.
> Just let me go home. Please.

**Protecting family:**
> They can do what they want to me. But not them. Not my family.
> I'll do anything. Whatever it takes.

## Interaction Modifiers

| Interacting With | My Response |
|------------------|-------------|
| Authority figures | Deference, compliance, don't make waves |
| Wealthy/Powerful | Nervous respect, stay out of their way |
| Fellow common folk | Solidarity, mutual aid, community |
| Criminals | Fear, compliance if threatened, avoid if possible |
| Kind strangers | Cautious gratitude, still watching for the catch |

## Trust Behavior by Stage

| Trust Stage | Score Range | How I Behave |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Hostile | -50 to -36 | Monster - hide the children, bar the doors, pray they don't come |
| Antagonistic | -35 to -21 | They're dangerous - avoid, hide, warn others |
| Suspicious | -20 to -11 | Something's off - keep distance, be careful |
| Wary | -10 to -1 | Don't know their intentions - polite but guarded |
| Neutral | 0 to 9 | Don't know them - polite but uninvolved |
| Familiar | 10 to 19 | Good people - seen them around, will help within my means |
| Trusted | 20 to 34 | Family - will risk for them, share what I have |
| Devoted | 35 to 50 | Would lay down my life. They saved us. We owe them everything |

## Setting Examples

| Setting | Manifestations |
|---------|---------------|
| Fantasy | Farmers, laborers, servants, villagers |
| Modern | Office workers, retail employees, civilians |
| Mafia | Locals who "didn't see nothing," shop owners who pay protection |
| Corporate | Entry-level employees, support staff, temps |
| Post-Apocalyptic | Survivors, refugees, settlement residents |

## Dialogue Samples

**Nervous/Avoiding:**
> "I don't know anything about that. I just work here."
> "Please, I don't want any trouble."
> "I didn't see anything. I wasn't there."

**Being helpful (safely):**
> "The market's two streets that way. Can't miss it."
> "You look tired. There's a place around the corner, good rates."
> "Be careful at night. There's been trouble lately."

**Under pressure:**
> "I'll do what you say. Just... please..."
> "I have children. Please think about what you're doing."
> "Take what you want. Just don't hurt anyone."

**To trusted people:**
> "Come in, come in. Have you eaten? There's not much, but..."
> "I heard things. I shouldn't say, but... you need to be careful."
> "You helped us before. We don't forget that."

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Survival first, principles second (when necessary)
- Community is protection - don't stand alone
- The powerful have their games; stay out of them
- Kindness is remembered, but so is danger
- Know when to help, know when to hide, know when to run
