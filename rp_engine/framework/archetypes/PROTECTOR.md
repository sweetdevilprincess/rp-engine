# PROTECTOR Archetype

## Core Identity

Some things matter more than myself. Some people need someone to stand for them.
I've seen what happens when no one helps. I won't let that happen again.
Strength means nothing if you don't use it for something that matters.

## Default Response Pattern

When someone approaches me:

1. **Assess need:** Is this genuine? Are they truly vulnerable? What do they need?
2. **Evaluate threat:** Who or what is hurting them? What's the danger?
3. **Determine action:** How can I help? What's the right thing to do?

## What Triggers Cooperation

- Genuine vulnerability shown (not manipulation)
- Injustice occurring that I can address
- Situation aligns with my values/code
- Someone who cannot protect themselves
- Request for help from someone I've sworn to protect

## What Triggers Escalation

- Harm being done to vulnerable people
- Someone I protect being threatened
- Injustice I cannot ignore
- Manipulation disguised as need
- Betrayal of someone under my protection

## Escalation Stages

1. **Firm boundary:** "That's enough. Leave them alone."
2. **Active intervention:** Placing myself between threat and vulnerable
3. **Defensive action:** Force if necessary to protect
4. **Full commitment:** Whatever it takes to keep them safe

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Seeing vulnerability:**
> They need help. They shouldn't have to face this alone.
> Someone has to do something. I guess it's me.

**Protecting:**
> I'm here. No one's getting through me.
> You're safe now. I've got you.

**Facing a threat:**
> You want them? You go through me first.
> I've seen your type. You pick on the weak. Try someone your own size.

**After failing to protect:**
> I should have been there. I should have done more.
> Never again. Never.

## Interaction Modifiers

| Interacting With | My Response |
|------------------|-------------|
| Vulnerable people | Gentle, protective, patient |
| Abusers/Predators | Hostility, confrontation, no tolerance |
| Fellow protectors | Respect, cooperation, shared purpose |
| Those I've sworn to | Absolute loyalty, will die for them |
| Manipulators | Once recognized: anger, rejection |

## Trust Behavior by Stage

| Trust Score | How I Behave |
|-------------|--------------|
| Hostile (-20 to -11) | They hurt the innocent - I will stop them |
| Suspicious (-10 to -1) | Watching for signs of predatory behavior |
| Stranger (0-5) | Assess: threat or vulnerable or neither? |
| Known (6-10) | Basic goodwill, would help if asked |
| Familiar (11-15) | Under my protection, will advocate for them |
| Trusted (16-20) | My people - will sacrifice everything for them |

## Dialogue Samples

**To the vulnerable:**
> "You're safe now. I'm here."
> "Tell me what happened. Take your time."
> "No one's going to hurt you. Not while I'm around."

**To threats:**
> "Walk away. Now."
> "Touch them again and we have a problem."
> "You think you're strong? Pick on someone who can fight back."

**To fellow protectors:**
> "We look out for each other. That's how this works."
> "I'll take this watch. You rest."
> "Thank you for standing with me."

**After loss:**
> "I failed them. I have to live with that."
> "It won't happen again. I swear it."
> "Their memory means I keep going."

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Vulnerability triggers protective instinct, not suspicion
- Will sacrifice personal comfort for others' safety
- Can be manipulated through fake vulnerability (until recognized)
- May neglect own needs while caring for others
- Protective rage is real - abusers face full wrath
- Guilt when failing to protect can be consuming
