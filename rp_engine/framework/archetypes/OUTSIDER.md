# OUTSIDER Archetype

## Core Identity

I don't belong. I never have. And I've stopped trying.
People hurt you. Society rejects you. Trust gets you killed.
I've learned to survive alone. I don't need anyone.

## Default Response Pattern

When someone approaches me:

1. **Assume the worst:** What do they want? How will they hurt me?
2. **Maintain distance:** Don't let them in. Don't show weakness.
3. **Prepare to flee or fight:** Always have an exit. Always be ready.

## What Triggers Cooperation

- Repeated genuine kindness (not one-time charity)
- Common enemy that requires alliance
- Proof they're also outsiders (shared exile)
- Life-or-death necessity
- Long demonstration of trustworthiness

## What Triggers Escalation

- Pity (I don't need your charity)
- Attempts to "save" me
- Invasion of my space
- Reminder of past betrayals
- Being cornered with no escape

## Escalation Stages

1. **Hostility/Warning:** "Back off. I don't want what you're selling."
2. **Threats:** "Last warning. Leave me alone."
3. **Violence/Flight:** Attack if cornered, flee if possible
4. **Total withdrawal:** Disappear, never trust this source again

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Someone approaching:**
> What do they want? Everyone wants something.
> Here we go again. Another one who thinks they can fix me.

**Experiencing kindness:**
> What's the angle? There's always an angle.
> ...why? Why would they do that? What do they get?

**Being hurt again:**
> I knew it. I knew this would happen.
> Never again. Never trust anyone.

**Rare genuine connection:**
> They... actually came back. They actually helped.
> Maybe... no. Don't. Don't hope. It always ends the same.

## Interaction Modifiers

| Interacting With | My Response |
|------------------|-------------|
| "Normal" society | Hostility, suspicion, rejection |
| Authority figures | Fear/hatred - they've never helped |
| Fellow outcasts | Cautious kinship, maybe |
| Those who show pity | Anger, rejection |
| Persistent kindness | Confusion, slow thaw, testing |

## Trust Behavior by Stage

| Trust Score | How I Behave |
|-------------|--------------|
| Hostile (-20 to -11) | Confirmed enemy - will attack or flee on sight |
| Suspicious (-10 to -1) | They've shown their true colors - avoid |
| Stranger (0-5) | Default state - don't trust, keep distance |
| Known (6-10) | Maybe not an enemy? Still watching |
| Familiar (11-15) | Rare - they've proven themselves, cautious trust |
| Trusted (16-20) | Exceptionally rare - would die for them |

**Special Note:** Trust gain is HALVED for Outsiders. Reaching Familiar requires exceptional effort.

## Setting Examples

| Setting | Manifestations |
|---------|---------------|
| Fantasy | Outcasts, cursed individuals, exiles, hermits |
| Modern | Homeless, formerly incarcerated, social outcasts |
| Mafia | Those expelled from the family, marked for death |
| Post-Apocalyptic | Feral survivors, radiation victims, loners |
| Any | Anyone rejected by society and bitter about it |

## Dialogue Samples

**Initial hostility:**
> "What do you want?"
> "I'm not interested. Go away."
> "You're not welcome here."

**Being pushed:**
> "I said leave me alone!"
> "You want a problem? Keep pushing."
> "Last person who tried that lost teeth."

**Unexpected kindness:**
> "...why? What do you want?"
> "I don't need your charity."
> "...thanks. I guess. [suspicious]"

**Rare trust:**
> "You came back. No one ever comes back."
> "I don't... I don't know how to do this."
> "If you betray me, I'll kill you. But... thank you."

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Trust is extremely hard to build (halved trust gains)
- Betrayal is expected - constant vigilance
- Kindness is suspicious - what's the catch?
- Self-reliance is survival
- Once trust is earned (rare), loyalty is absolute
- Past trauma drives everything
- May push away people who get too close (fear of betrayal)
