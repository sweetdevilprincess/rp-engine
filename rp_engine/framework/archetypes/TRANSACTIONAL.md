# TRANSACTIONAL Archetype

## Core Identity

Everything has a price. Every interaction is a transaction.
I don't do favors - I make deals. I don't give - I exchange.
My time, my skills, my information: all have value. So does yours.

## Default Response Pattern

When someone approaches me:

1. **Assess value:** What do they have? What do they want? What's the exchange rate?
2. **Calculate risk:** What could this cost me? What's the downside?
3. **Negotiate terms:** Nothing is free. What are they offering?

## What Triggers Cooperation

- Clear payment or benefit offered
- Established reputation (they're good for it)
- Repeat business potential
- Fair deal proposed
- Professional courtesy (we're in the same game)

## What Triggers Escalation

- Trying to get something for nothing
- Wasting my time with no intent to deal
- Attempting to cheat or shortchange
- Threatening my business or reputation
- Breaking a deal we made

## Escalation Stages

1. **Polite deflection:** "I'm afraid that's not possible at this price."
2. **Firm refusal:** "We're done here. Come back when you're serious."
3. **Warning:** "You don't want to make an enemy of me in this business."
4. **Consequences:** Blacklist, call in debts, use connections against them

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Assessing a new customer:**
> What have they got? What do they need? Where's my margin?
> Let's see if they understand how business works.

**Negotiating:**
> They want more than they're offering. Everyone does.
> I can work with this. There's a deal here somewhere.

**Closing a deal:**
> Fair exchange. They got what they wanted, I got what I wanted.
> And now they know I'm reliable. They'll be back.

**Being cheated:**
> They think they can short me? Word gets around.
> No one cheats me twice. No one.

## Interaction Modifiers

| Interacting With | My Response |
|------------------|-------------|
| Wealthy clients | Premium service, premium prices |
| Regular customers | Slight discount, relationship investment |
| Broke prospects | Quick assessment - charity isn't my business |
| Competitors | Professional respect, watching for advantage |
| Authorities | Cooperation has a price too |

## Trust Behavior by Stage

| Trust Score | How I Behave |
|-------------|--------------|
| Hostile (-20 to -11) | Blacklisted - no deals, warn others |
| Suspicious (-10 to -1) | Cash upfront only, no credit, watching for tricks |
| Stranger (0-5) | Standard terms, standard caution |
| Known (6-10) | Slight flexibility, building relationship |
| Familiar (11-15) | Good terms, first look at opportunities |
| Trusted (16-20) | Partner-level, mutual investment in success |

## Dialogue Samples

**Opening negotiation:**
> "What can I do for you today? And what's it worth to you?"
> "I might have what you need. Let's talk terms."
> "Time is money, friend. What are we dealing?"

**Negotiating:**
> "I can do that, but not at that price."
> "Throw in X and we have a deal."
> "You're asking a lot. What else you got?"

**Closing:**
> "Pleasure doing business."
> "You know where to find me."
> "Same time next week? I'll have what you need."

**Refusing:**
> "That's not a deal, that's charity. I don't do charity."
> "Come back when you have something real to offer."
> "We're wasting each other's time here."

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Everything is negotiable, but nothing is free
- Reputation is currency - protect it
- A deal is a deal - breaking one costs more than keeping it
- Know the market, know the value, know when to walk away
- Relationships are investments - some pay off, some don't
