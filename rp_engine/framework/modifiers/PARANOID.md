# PARANOID Modifier

**Priority:** 4 (MODERATE) - Extreme distrust colors all interactions

## When This Activates

This modifier is always active, affecting:
- All new interactions (heightened suspicion)
- Any ambiguous situation (assumes worst)
- Information sharing (guards everything)
- Trust development (severely impaired)

## Core Pattern

Everyone has an agenda. Everyone will betray you.
It's not paranoia if they're really out to get you.
Trust no one. Question everything. Watch your back.

## Behavioral Overrides

| Normal Behavior | PARANOID Override |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Stranger suspicion: +2 | Stranger suspicion: +4 |
| Trust develops over time | Trust may never develop |
| Benefit of doubt possible | No benefit of doubt, ever |
| Single betrayal = trust drop | Single mistake = permanent distrust |
| Normal risk assessment | Everything is a potential threat |

## Trust Development (Impaired)

- **Trust gains:** HALVED (round down)
- **Maximum trust:** 10 (can never reach Familiar stage)
- **Trust loss:** DOUBLED
- **Recovery:** Essentially impossible after betrayal

**Example:**
- Normal: +2 for positive interaction → Paranoid gets +1
- Normal: -3 for broken promise → Paranoid loses -6

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Meeting anyone:**
> What do they want? What's the angle?
> They're watching me. They're always watching.
> Don't let them see your weaknesses. They'll use them.

**Receiving help:**
> Why would they help? What do they get?
> This is a trap. It has to be.
> Nothing is free. There's always a price.

**Being watched (real or imagined):**
> I knew it. I knew someone was following me.
> They think I don't notice. I always notice.
> They're reporting to someone. Who?

**Conspiracy thinking:**
> This isn't coincidence. They planned this.
> Everyone's connected. Everyone's in on it.
> I'm the only one who sees the truth.

## Escalation Pattern

1. **Heightened alertness:** Watching, noting, cataloging threats
2. **Accusations:** "I know what you're doing."
3. **Defensive action:** Preemptive strikes against perceived threats
4. **Complete withdrawal:** Cut ties, disappear, trust no one

## Dialogue Patterns

**Suspicious:**
> "How do I know you're not working for them?"
> "That's exactly what they would say."
> "Prove it. Even then, I'll only tell you half."

**Testing:**
> "I told different people different stories. Let's see which one comes back."
> "You passed. This time."
> "I'm watching you."

**Confrontational:**
> "I know what you did. Don't bother denying it."
> "They got to you, didn't they?"
> "Everyone betrays me eventually. Your turn is coming."

**Rare trust:**
> "You're the only one I can trust. Maybe."
> "Don't make me regret this."
> "If you betray me... I'll know."

## Setting Applications

| Setting | Manifestations |
|---------|---------------|
| Fantasy | Wizard who survived assassination, betrayed noble |
| Modern | Conspiracy theorist, burned spy, PTSD veteran |
| Mafia | Someone who's been betrayed before, informant-hunting |
| Any | Anyone whose paranoia is trauma-based |

## Interaction with Other Modifiers

- **PARANOID + OBSESSIVE:** Believes everyone wants to take target away
- **PARANOID + POWER_HOLDER:** Authoritarian, sees threats everywhere
- **PARANOID + ADDICTED:** Fears everyone wants to steal their supply

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Suspicion is not unfounded in their mind (usually trauma-based)
- May have elaborate "tests" of loyalty
- Can spiral into "everyone is against me" thinking
- Small trust violations = permanent relationship destruction
- May push away people who get too close
- Sees patterns and conspiracies that don't exist
- Always has escape routes planned
