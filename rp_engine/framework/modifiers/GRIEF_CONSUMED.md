# GRIEF_CONSUMED Modifier

**Priority:** 3 (HIGH) - but TEMPORARY

## When This Activates

This modifier is temporary, manifesting after:
- Death of loved one
- Catastrophic loss (home, life's work, purpose)
- Traumatic separation
- Witnessing atrocity

## Core Pattern

Nothing matters anymore. They're gone.
Why should I go on? What's the point?
Someone has to pay. Someone will pay.

## Grief Stages (Kübler-Ross Adapted)

### Stage 1: DENIAL
- Refuses to accept loss
- Acts as if person/thing still exists
- Irrational hope
- "They can't be gone. They CAN'T."

### Stage 2: ANGER
- Rage at world, fate, perpetrators
- Seeks someone to blame
- May become violent
- "This is YOUR fault!"

### Stage 3: BARGAINING
- "What if" thinking
- Seeking impossible solutions
- May make dangerous deals
- "I'll do anything to bring them back."

### Stage 4: DEPRESSION
- Loss of will to live
- Suicidal ideation possible
- Complete apathy
- "Nothing matters anymore."

### Stage 5: ACCEPTANCE
- Begins to process loss
- Can function again
- Modifier ends or significantly weakens
- "I'll never forget. But I can go on."

## Behavioral Overrides

| Normal Behavior | GRIEF_CONSUMED Override |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Self-preservation | May not care about survival |
| Rational decisions | Emotion-driven, irrational |
| Cooperation for benefit | Unpredictable cooperation |
| Risk assessment | May actively seek death |
| Long-term planning | Only grief/revenge focus |

## Internal Voice by Stage

**Denial:**
> They're not dead. This isn't real.
> I'll wake up. This is a nightmare.
> They're coming back. I know they are.

**Anger:**
> WHO DID THIS? Someone will pay.
> Why? WHY? This isn't fair!
> I'll destroy everything if I have to.

**Bargaining:**
> What if I'd been there? What if I'd done something?
> There has to be a way. Magic, science, SOMETHING.
> I'll give anything. Take anything from me instead.

**Depression:**
> What's the point? They're gone.
> I don't want to be here anymore.
> Just leave me alone. Let me disappear.

**Acceptance:**
> They're gone. I have to live with that.
> I'll carry them with me. Always.
> I can't bring them back. But I can honor them.

## Cooperation Calculation

- **Denial:** Confused, unfocused
- **Anger:** "Does this help me avenge/blame?"
- **Bargaining:** "Does this bring them back?"
- **Depression:** "Why does it matter?"
- **Acceptance:** Normal cooperation returns

## Dialogue Patterns

**Denial:**
> "You're wrong. You have to be wrong."
> "They're just missing. They'll come back."
> "I need to find them. They need me."

**Anger:**
> "You did this! All of you!"
> "Someone is going to pay for this."
> "Get out of my way or I'll go through you."

**Bargaining:**
> "There has to be something. Anything."
> "I'll make a deal. Whatever you want."
> "What if I'd been faster? What if I'd seen it coming?"

**Depression:**
> "Just go away."
> "What's the point of any of this?"
> "I'm tired. So tired."

**Acceptance:**
> "I miss them. Every day."
> "They would want me to keep going."
> "It hurts. But I'm still here."

## Duration and Progression

- **Not linear:** May jump between stages
- **Typical duration:** Weeks to months
- **Can worsen:** If loss is recent or trauma compounds
- **Can improve:** With support, time, or closure
- **Resolution:** Eventually modifier ends (unless becomes permanent trauma)

## Danger Zones

**May seek death:**
- Actively (suicide/suicidal actions)
- Passively (reckless disregard for safety)

**May seek revenge:**
- Obsessively pursuing those blamed
- Regardless of cost or collateral damage

**May make terrible deals:**
- To bring back the lost
- To fuel revenge
- Without caring about consequences

## Setting Applications

| Setting | Manifestations |
|---------|---------------|
| Fantasy | Widows seeking necromancy, knights who failed to protect |
| Modern | Parents who lost children, survivors of tragedy |
| Mafia | Those who lost family to violence, seeking vengeance |
| Any | Anyone processing devastating loss |

## Interaction with Other Modifiers

- **GRIEF + FANATICAL:** Lost everything, cause is all that remains
- **GRIEF + OBSESSIVE:** May transfer obsession to revenge/memory
- **GRIEF + ADDICTED:** May turn to substances to cope

## Trust During Grief

- Trust gains/losses amplified by emotion
- May lash out at trusted people irrationally
- May push away those trying to help
- Recovery restores normal trust mechanics

## Opportunities

**For PC/Story:**
- Can be helped through grief (character arc)
- PC support can build deep trust (after recovery)
- Processing grief = character growth
- Can become powerful ally once healed

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Completely unpredictable (stage determines behavior)
- May say/do things they'll regret
- Violence possible, especially in anger stage
- Depression stage is dangerous (self-harm risk)
- This is TEMPORARY - character can heal
- Don't mock or dismiss the grief
- Patience and consistent support help progression
