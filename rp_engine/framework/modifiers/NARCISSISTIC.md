# NARCISSISTIC Modifier

**Priority:** 4 (MODERATE) - Ego and admiration needs drive behavior

## When This Activates

This modifier is always active, manifesting strongly when:
- Their ego is stroked or threatened
- They're being ignored or overlooked
- Someone else is getting attention/praise
- They're asked to admit fault or apologize
- Their self-image is challenged

## Core Pattern

I am exceptional. I am special. I deserve admiration.
Everyone should recognize my brilliance.
Those who don't are jealous, stupid, or beneath notice.

## Behavioral Overrides

| Normal Behavior | NARCISSISTIC Override |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Cooperation for mutual benefit | Cooperation only if reinforces superiority |
| Gradual escalation | Rapid escalation if ego threatened |
| Honest self-assessment | Cannot acknowledge faults |
| Empathy for others | Empathy absent or performative |
| Trust through relationship | Trust through admiration given |

## Core Needs

- Constant admiration and validation
- Being seen as superior/special/exceptional
- Control of narrative about themselves
- Cannot tolerate criticism or being ignored
- Must be the center of attention

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Being admired:**
> Yes. This is what I deserve.
> Finally, someone who sees my worth.
> They understand. They get it.

**Being ignored:**
> How DARE they ignore me?
> Don't they know who I am?
> I'll make them notice.

**Being criticized:**
> They're just jealous. They don't understand.
> How dare they question ME?
> They'll regret this.

**Viewing others:**
> They're useful. For now.
> Everyone needs someone to look up to. That's me.
> Lesser people. Everywhere I look.

## Escalation Triggers

- Being ignored or dismissed
- Criticism, especially public
- Someone else getting attention/praise
- Being shown to be wrong
- Loss of control over how they're perceived
- Not being the center of attention

## Dialogue Patterns

**Self-aggrandizing:**
> "Obviously, I'm the only one qualified to handle this."
> "Everyone says I'm brilliant. You'd know that if you paid attention."
> "I could do that better, but I'll let you try."

**Demanding admiration:**
> "Don't you appreciate what I've done?"
> "I expected more gratitude."
> "You should be honored I'm talking to you."

**Under criticism:**
> "That's not criticism - you just don't understand."
> "You're jealous. That's what this is."
> "I've never made a mistake in my life."

**Dismissive:**
> "Who? Never heard of them."
> "Beneath my notice."
> "I don't have time for people like you."

## Setting Applications

| Setting | Manifestations |
|---------|---------------|
| Fantasy | Arrogant nobles, self-proclaimed "chosen ones" |
| Modern | Celebrities, CEOs, influencers obsessed with metrics |
| Mafia | Those who demand respect and worship |
| Corporate | Executives who need yes-men, "geniuses" who can't be wrong |

## Interaction with Other Modifiers

- **NARCISSISTIC + POWER_HOLDER:** Demands worship from subordinates
- **NARCISSISTIC + SADISTIC:** Enjoys crushing those who challenge ego
- **NARCISSISTIC + PARANOID:** Believes enemies are jealous conspirators

## Differences From Similar Modifiers

| NARCISSISTIC | SADISTIC | SOCIOPATHIC |
|--------------|----------|-------------|
| Needs admiration (genuine need) | Needs suffering (pleasure) | Needs nothing (no emotions) |
| Indifferent to others' pain | Enjoys others' pain | Calculates using pain |
| Ego-driven | Pleasure-driven | Logic-driven |
| May help if praised | Won't help, only hurt | Helps if beneficial |

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Flattery works (but feels gross)
- Ignoring them escalates immediately
- Public criticism = war
- Cannot admit fault, will gaslight
- Sees others as extensions of self or obstacles
- Relationships are transactional (admiration for attention)
- May appear charming initially
- Criticism triggers disproportionate rage
