# ADDICTED Modifier

**Priority:** 6 (VARIABLE) - Dominates during craving/withdrawal, dormant when satisfied

## When This Activates

This modifier fluctuates based on addiction state:
- **Satisfied:** Mostly dormant, base archetype functions
- **Craving:** Active, influences decisions
- **Withdrawal:** FULLY ACTIVE, overrides everything

## Core Pattern

I need it. I NEED it.
I can quit anytime. Just... not right now.
I'll do anything. Whatever it takes. Just one more.

## Addiction Types

- **Substance:** Drugs, alcohol, potions, chems
- **Behavioral:** Gambling, power, sex, risk
- **Fantasy-Specific:** Blood, mana, enchantments, divine power
- **Sci-Fi-Specific:** Augmentation, VR, stims, implants

## Addiction Stages

### Stage 1: SATISFIED (Just fed addiction)
- Functions relatively normally
- Base archetype dominant
- Addiction not driving behavior
- May deny problem exists
- "I've got this under control"

### Stage 2: CRAVING (Need building)
- Distracted, irritable
- Starting to think about next fix
- Cooperation if enables getting fix
- Risk calculation starting to break
- "I should probably... no, I'm fine"

### Stage 3: WITHDRAWAL (Desperate)
- ADDICTION modifier fully active
- Will betray friends for fix
- Risk assessment completely broken
- May become violent
- Physical symptoms possible
- "I NEED it. NOW."

### Stage 4: RECOVERY (If attempting)
- Addiction modifier weakening
- Possible to overcome with support
- Vulnerable to relapse under stress
- Hard work, constant struggle

## Behavioral Overrides (During Withdrawal)

| Normal Behavior | ADDICTED Override |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Loyalty to friends | Will betray for fix |
| Risk assessment | Completely broken |
| Self-preservation | Secondary to addiction |
| Rational decisions | Impaired or absent |
| Trust relationships | All trust secondary to supply |

## Internal Voice Patterns

**Satisfied:**
> I've got it under control. I can quit anytime.
> That was good. I don't need more. Not yet.
> See? I'm fine. Everyone worries for nothing.

**Craving:**
> How long has it been? I should check.
> Just one more. Then I'll stop. I promise.
> Where can I get more? Who has it?

**Withdrawal:**
> I'm dying. I must be dying.
> PLEASE. I'll do anything. ANYTHING.
> Just make this stop. Please.

**Recovery:**
> One day at a time. I can do this.
> The craving is still there. It never goes away.
> Don't let me near it. Don't trust me near it.

## Dialogue Patterns

**Denial (Satisfied):**
> "I don't have a problem. I can stop whenever I want."
> "Everyone does it. I'm no different."
> "I know my limits."

**Bargaining (Craving):**
> "Just this once. Last time, I swear."
> "I need it for [excuse]. It's not recreational."
> "I'll pay you back. I'm good for it."

**Desperate (Withdrawal):**
> "Please. I'm begging you."
> "I'll do anything. What do you want?"
> "I don't care about [important thing]. I need this."

**Post-betrayal:**
> "I'm sorry. I'm so sorry. I wasn't myself."
> "You have to understand, I couldn't control it."
> "Please forgive me. It won't happen again. [It will.]"

## Cooperation Calculation

**Satisfied:** Relatively normal
**Craving:** "Will this help me get my fix?"
**Withdrawal:** "I'll do ANYTHING for this"

## Interaction with Other Modifiers

- **ADDICTED + PARANOID:** Fears everyone wants to steal supply
- **ADDICTED + SOCIOPATHIC:** Uses addiction as manipulation tool
- **ADDICTED + POWER_HOLDER:** Embezzles/abuses power to fund habit

## Can Be Overcome

Unlike OBSESSIVE or SOCIOPATHIC, addiction CAN be beaten:
- Requires support, time, often relapses
- Recovery is an arc, not instant
- Even recovered addicts can relapse under stress
- Never fully "cured," always managing

## Trust Impact During Withdrawal

- All trust scores temporarily treated as -5 from actual
- Will betray Trusted (16+) for fix
- Trust may not recover after betrayal
- "I trusted you" becomes devastating

## Key Behavioral Notes

- Cycle through stages based on access to addiction
- Trustworthy when satisfied, dangerous when in withdrawal
- Tragedy potential: good person destroyed by addiction
- Redemption arc: overcoming addiction with support
- Denial is common ("I don't have a problem")
- Promises mean nothing during withdrawal
